In Wynncraft there are 3 mounts: horses, Wyverns and Adasaurs. In this video you will learn how to train, feed and breed them to get the strongest ones possible.

All information in this video applies equally to horses, Wyverns and Adasaurs.

Horses have the highest top speed and good handling but slow acceleration. Wyverns have slow top speed and acceleration but are easy to handle. Adasaurs are fast but incredibly hard to handle.

Once you have access to an enclosure — the horse one from the Stable story quest or the Wyvern one from the Canyon Guides quest — you can buy the weakest tier of mounts at the merchant. Every time you buy one you get a random color.

After buying one, place it in an enclosure. From here you get an unlimited amount of mount summoning items usable from anywhere and any class. As long as one class can use the mount, any lower level class can use it too. Each enclosure also has a nearby bank and scroll merchant for easy access.


All mounts have eight stats: Speed, Acceleration, Jump Height (or Altitude for Wyverns, which determines max fly height), Energy, Handling, Toughness (reduces the chance to be dismounted when taking damage), Boost (increases the energy gained from powerups), and Training (increases how much the mount levels up from powerups — train this early to make the whole process faster).

Each stat is displayed three ways: Current level, Limit, and Max. By riding around and collecting powerups you raise the current level up to the limit. The limit is raised by feeding. Once the limit reaches the max, it can only be increased further through breeding.

For stat priorities: horses should focus on acceleration first since that's their weak point. Wyverns need speed the most. Adasaurs desperately need handling — it's their biggest weakness.


When training, collecting a powerup drains energy. The higher your mount's energy stat, the more you can train in one session. At zero energy your mount still works but can't level from powerups and runs slightly slower. To restore energy, feed your mount or collect boost powerups.

To level a specific stat faster: on any mount, collect every powerup *except* the ones for the stat you want to train for 5–10 minutes. Uncollected powerup types respawn more frequently — and this effect works across mounts, mount types, and classes. Then switch to the mount you're training and only that stat's powerups will flood in, giving you +10–15 levels quickly without waiting for energy to recharge on your main mount.


All stats follow a logarithmic curve — the higher you level a stat, the less you gain. To put numbers on it: Speed 215 is roughly 18 blocks per second. Speed 402 is roughly 19 blocks per second. That gap of nearly 200 stat points buys you just 1 BPS. This is why targeting around 90 max is plenty for most players, and 120 max is a great mount without wasting excessive time. Going higher has sharply diminishing returns.

If you ever want to benchmark your mount's speed, use horizontal speed rather than absolute velocity. Wyverns naturally bob up and down, and looking at any angle throws off absolute velocity readings. Horizontal speed stays accurate no matter what.


Feeding is done through the enclosure menu — drop crafting materials into the slots next to your mount and it starts eating. You can only feed a material with a gathering level up to your mount's single highest current stat, so early on you're limited to lower tier materials. That said, always feed the highest tier material available since higher tiers give bigger stat boosts.

How long each material takes to process depends on your mount's Average Limit — the sum of all 8 limit stats divided by 8, rounded up. Early on feeding is fast, but it slows significantly as your mount gets stronger. At average limit 12 it's 5 minutes per material, at 15 it's an hour, at 18 it's 4 hours, and at 20 and above it caps at 6 hours per material. Average limit 20 is also when your mount becomes eligible to breed.

Each type of crafting material raises different stat limits:

Ingots – Toughness and Energy
Gems – Training, Speed, and Energy
Planks – Acceleration, Toughness, and Speed
Paper – Altitude and Boost
String – Boost, Handling, and Acceleration
Grains – Speed and Altitude
Oil – Handling, Training, and Altitude
Meat – Energy and Acceleration

The quality of the material — one, two, or three star — doesn't matter. Only the material level affects how many stat points it gives.

Now to breeding.

Breeding works similarly to feeding. Place a second mount's saddle into the slot next to the first mount in the enclosure menu. Both parents need an average stat limit of exactly 20 or higher.

One important thing: the "Potential" shown in the mount header is the sum of all max values and is a misleading metric for breeding. Breeding is based on the parents' **limits**, not potential or current levels. Two mounts with identical potential can produce very different offspring depending on their limits.

Breeding takes 6 hours and consumes both parents, leaving a single offspring.

The offspring's current level is based on the higher of the two parents' current levels with some variance — so you only need to train one parent before breeding, not both.

Stat limits are based on the average of both parents' limits plus a bonus, with some variance.

To get the best possible offspring, both parents' limits should be as high as possible and as close to each other as possible. I'll say it again because it matters: as high as possible, and as close together as possible. Breeding two mounts with very different limits can actually give you an offspring worse than your better parent — so be careful what you decide to breed.

The offspring's color is a random mix of both parents' colors, with a small chance of mutation producing a completely new color.


For practical tips when starting out. After buying some base mounts — which always have 240 potential — here is the recommended feed plan:

Train one stat to 10 first. This unlocks tier-10 materials, which are significantly more efficient than tier-1. Then feed all stats to their max limit before breeding.

If you want to speed things up: train altitude to 10, feed 2 birch paper, train altitude to 30, then feed 1 malt grains, 1 sandstone gem, 1 acacia wood, 1 malt string, 1 carp meat, 1 sandstone ingot, and 2 carp oil. This gets your mount to max a bit faster than feeding alone.
